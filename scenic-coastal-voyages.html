â“ Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a gulet and a yacht?

Gulet: Traditional Turkish/Greek wooden sailing boat, wide beam, emphasis on deck space and stability. More rustic charm.
Yacht: Modern sailing or motor vessel, sleeker design, often faster. More contemporary luxury.

Do I need sailing experience?

No! All vessels come with professional captain and crew. You can help hoist sails if interested, or just relax.

What about seasickness?

Coastal cruising stays close to shore with calmer waters than open ocean. Most gulets/small ships have stabilizers. Travel in shoulder seasons (calmer seas). Bring medication just in case.

Can we customize the itinerary?

On private charters: absolutely! Captain will suggest routes based on weather and your interests. On shared cruises: route is set, but some flexibility for swimming/dining stops.

Are these cruises family-friendly?

Greek gulets and Turkish blue cruises: very family-friendly. Nile dahabiyas and Mekong cruises: better for older children (10+). Norwegian Hurtigruten: great for all ages.
