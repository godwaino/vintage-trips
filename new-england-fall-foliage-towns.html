1. Stowe, Vermont

Peak: Late September to early October
Iconic Inn: Trapp Family Lodge‚Äîyes, the real Von Trapp family
Must-See View: Mt. Mansfield gondola ride
Why Visit: Quintessential mountain village with covered bridges, maple syrup farms, and alpine scenery that rivals European destinations

2. Woodstock, Vermont

Peak: Early to mid-October
Iconic Inn: Woodstock Inn & Resort
Must-See View: Billings Farm & Museum overlooking rolling pastures
Why Visit: One of America's prettiest towns‚Äîwhite church steeples, covered bridges, and a village green straight from a Norman Rockwell painting

3. Bennington, Vermont

Peak: Late September to early October
Iconic Inn: The Equinox Resort & Spa (nearby Manchester)
Must-See View: Bennington Battle Monument
Why Visit: Revolutionary War history meets leaf-peeping paradise. Perfect stop between southern Vermont and the Berkshires

4. Stockbridge, Massachusetts

Peak: Mid-October
Iconic Inn: The Red Lion Inn
Must-See View: Main Street at sunset (this IS the Norman Rockwell painting)
Why Visit: Home to Norman Rockwell Museum and classic New England architecture. Pair with nearby Lenox for Tanglewood grounds

5. Litchfield, Connecticut

Peak: Mid to late October
Iconic Inn: Winvian Farm (luxury farm cottages)
Must-See View: White Memorial Conservation Center trails
Why Visit: Connecticut's prettiest town‚Äîhistoric district, antique shops, and fewer crowds than Vermont hotspots

6. Camden, Maine

Peak: Early to mid-October
Iconic Inn: Camden Harbour Inn
Must-See View: Mt. Battie summit (drive or hike) overlooking Penobscot Bay
Why Visit: Where mountains meet the sea. Combine fall foliage with coastal charm, lobster shacks, and schooner cruises

7. Franconia, New Hampshire

Peak: Late September to early October
Iconic Inn: Omni Mount Washington Resort (Bretton Woods)
Must-See View: Franconia Notch State Park‚Äîdrive through a tunnel of color
Why Visit: White Mountains drama with fewer tourists than North Conway. Historic hotel setting rivals European grand resorts

8. Jackson, New Hampshire

Peak: Late September to early October
Iconic Inn: The Wentworth (Victorian elegance)
Must-See View: Jackson Covered Bridge with Mt. Washington backdrop
Why Visit: New England's most photographed covered bridge plus access to Mt. Washington Auto Road

9. Kennebunkport, Maine

Peak: Mid-October
Iconic Inn: The Colony Hotel
Must-See View: Ocean Avenue coastal drive
Why Visit: Southern Maine coast meets autumn color. Add lobster rolls and lighthouse visits to your leaf-peeping agenda

10. Newport, Rhode Island

Peak: Late October
Iconic Inn: Castle Hill Inn
Must-See View: Cliff Walk with mansion views
Why Visit: Gilded Age mansions framed by fall foliage. Extend the season with coastal elegance when inland areas have peaked
