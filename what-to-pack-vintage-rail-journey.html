What to Pack for a Vintage Rail Journey (Without Overpacking)

Smart casual dress code, compact cabin essentials, and analog touches for the Orient Express and other luxury heritage trains.

Understand Your Cabin and Dress Code

Heritage trains like the Venice Simplon-Orient-Express have specific expectations. Your packing depends on:

  • Cabin type: Historic cabins (VSOE) = limited luggage space. Modern luxury (Rocky Mountaineer) = more room.
  • Dress code: VSOE requires formal attire for dinner. Japanese trains (Seven Stars) are smart casual. African Rovos Rail = safari chic.
  • Journey length: 1-night trips (Paris-Venice) vs. 4-night adventures (Cape Town-Pretoria) change your needs.

Core Clothing: Smart but Comfortable

For Orient Express and European Heritage Trains

Daytime:

  • Smart trousers or skirt
  • Cashmere sweater or blazer (trains can be cool)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for station stops
  • Scarf or wrap (doubles as blanket, style piece)

Evening (Formal Dining Car):

  • Men: Suit or tuxedo, dress shoes, tie
  • Women: Cocktail dress or evening gown, heels, jewelry

Overnight in Cabin:

  • Silk pajamas or robe (cabins convert to beds)
  • Slippers

For Rocky Mountaineer and Daytime Scenic Trains

  • Layers (temperature changes in mountains)
  • Dark jeans or chinos
  • Button-down shirt or blouse
  • Fleece or light jacket
  • No formal wear neededâ€”smart casual throughout

For African Heritage Trains (Rovos Rail)

  • Neutral safari colors (beige, khaki, olive)
  • Long sleeves for evening (mosquitoes)
  • Hat and sunglasses for viewing deck
  • One formal outfit for gala dinner

Compact Cabin Essentials

Space is limited. Prioritize multi-use items:

  • Toiletries in travel sizes: Most trains provide basics, but bring your preferences
  • Medications and prescriptions: No pharmacy stops mid-journey
  • Phone charger + adapter: European trains use EU plugs (type C/F)
  • Eye mask + earplugs: Even in deluxe cabins, train sounds vary
  • Refillable water bottle: Hydration matters in compact spaces
  • Small crossbody bag: For station stops and wandering dining cars

Analog Touches That Enhance the Experience

Heritage trains are about slowing down. Leave the laptop, embrace the era:

  • A real book or journal: Perfect for gazing out windows between chapters
  • Film camera or vintage-style digital: Match the aesthetic
  • Playing cards or travel games: Cabins are cozy for two
  • Fountain pen and stationery: Write postcards to mail at station stops

What NOT to Pack

  • Large rolling suitcases: Hard to maneuver in narrow corridors. Soft duffels or vintage suitcases work better.
  • Excessive electronics: You won't need a laptop, tablet, AND e-reader. Pick one.
  • Bulky shoes: 2 pairs max (one for day, one for evening). Trains have limited under-bed storage.
  • Work materials: If you can't truly unplug, reconsider the trip.

Luggage Strategy

Orient Express: One carry-on size bag per person (20-24 inches). Check larger bags to be delivered to your hotel.
Rocky Mountaineer: 2 bags per person (stored separately). Bring a daypack for the train.
Rovos Rail: Generous allowance, but soft-sided luggage preferred for cabin storage.

Special Considerations

Photography Gear

Trains have phenomenal views. Bring:

  • Camera with versatile zoom (24-70mm or 18-200mm)
  • Lens cloth (windows get fingerprints)
  • Extra batteries (not all cabins have abundant outlets)

Weather Prep

European trains cross climates quickly. Venice in July = hot. Swiss Alps same day = chilly. Layers are non-negotiable.

Cultural Sensitivity

Japanese luxury trains: Remove shoes in cabins (bring clean socks). European trains: Tipping is expected (cash in local currency).

Sample Packing List: 2-Night Orient Express Journey

  • Day 1: Travel outfit (smart trousers, sweater, loafers)
  • Evening 1: Formal attire for dinner
  • Day 2: Fresh daytime outfit for scenic viewing
  • Evening 2: Second formal option (or repeat with different accessories)
  • Extras: Pajamas, toiletries, book, camera, journal, scarf
  • Luggage: One soft-sided carry-on (22 inches) + small crossbody bag
